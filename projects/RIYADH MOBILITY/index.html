 RIYADH MOBILITY:

 PAINTING WITH DATA-RIYADH

Overview

The city of Riyadh is currently investing a large amount of resources in the construction of a metro system. The introduction of this new transportation system has repercussions that go beyond the physical infrastructure of the city: in a geographic region with complex political and social conditions, such an infrastructure has the capacity to disrupt and transform these relationships.

While the particular issues of transportation access that specific social groups face are well known, the datasets describing such situations are non-existent or scarce at best. In this project, we explore the use of bottom-up data collection through social media and web-scraping to make sense out of the region’s urban dynamics, providing agency to the inhabitants of the city in the construction of their urban representation. The project experiments with alternative data representations, where the construction of relationships among discrete spatial and non-spatial layers aims to discover the serendipitous relationships that bring urban life into being. Ultimately, through a combination of bottom-up datasets, crowd-sourced interfaces and top-down government information, we aim to construct a platform of urban knowledge, focusing on data computation, rather than data collection.
