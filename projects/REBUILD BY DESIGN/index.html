REBUILD BY DESIGN

Overview

Organized by the President’s Hurricane Sandy Rebuilding task force, Rebuild by Design is an international competition in which teams submit proposals addressing climate resilience and flood protection in vulnerable coastal regions in the northeast. The competition was launched in the summer of 2013 to develop multifaceted, innovative solutions to continuing threats resulting from climate change. In September 2013, MIT’s Center for Advanced Urbanism (CAU) was selected as one of ten interdisciplinary teams to advance to the next competition phase. Under CAU’s leadership, the Civic Data Design Lab partnered with a team of fellow researchers, engineers and designers from MIT and a group of Dutch firms that specialize in disaster resilient design. Their design proposal, "New Meadowlands", protects a unique area of approximately 30 square miles in the New York/New Jersey Metropolitan Region, simultaneously addressing issues of ecology, economy, and real estate development.

In June 2014, HUD secretary Shaun Donovan announced the team as one of six winners of Rebuild by Design. The $150 million reward will be used to provide flood protection for the first part of the proposed section, boosting the local economy, restoring the natural environment and protecting residents against future disasters.
